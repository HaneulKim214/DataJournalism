Correlations Discovered Between Health Risks and Age, Income

Dataset used to create plots above is from here and year 2014 has been used.

Click different x axis labels and y axis labels to see 9 different possible correlations

HealthCare Vs:

  1. Poverty Rate
    • There is definite positive relation ship between population lacking health care and poverty rate.
    • This maybe due to lack of hospitals around cities with high poverty rate or simply people not wanting to pay extra dollars on seeing doctors.
  2. Age
    • No clear correlation however one could better this plot by instead of taking mean age, take multiple age groups and find difference between them, and ask questions like which age group lacks healthcare the most.
  3. Household Income
    • Clear negative correlation. People with higher income tend to have better health care

Smoking Population Vs:

  1. Poverty Rate
    1. Surprisingly, cities with higher poverty rate has greater smoking population.
    2. Interesting fact is that south east cities tend to have greater poverty rate.
  2. Age
    1. No clear correlations
    2. Again, south east cities have greatest smoking populations
  3. Household Income
    1. Negative correlation similar to poverty rate vs. Household income.

Obese Population Vs:

  • Similar trend with other two Y-labels.

Conclusion

There are similarities between all the y-labels. Obese population, smoking population, and population that lack healthcare all seem to have similar trends that is as poverty rate increase all y-axis features increases and display negative correlation with household income.

Assumption that can be made is that people who live in high poverty cities are prone to smoking, bad diet, and therefore do not really care about their health.
